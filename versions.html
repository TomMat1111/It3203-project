Technical Evolution of Speed

While HTTP/1.1 was reliable, it suffered from "Head-of-Line Blocking." Your research highlights how HTTP/2 and HTTP/3 solved these issues.

Version Transport Layer Key Innovation
HTTP/2 TCP Multiplexing (Single Connection)
HTTP/3 QUIC (UDP) Reduced Handshake Latency

Detailed Analysis: Head-of-Line (HOL) Blocking

A primary research finding is the elimination of HOL blocking. In HTTP/1.1, a single large packet loss blocks the entire connection. HTTP/3 solves this via QUIC’s independent streams, ensuring that a lost packet in one stream doesn't stall others.

The Move to QUIC

HTTP/3 represents a fundamental re-engineering of the internet by moving from TCP to the UDP-based QUIC protocol, specifically designed for a mobile-first world.
